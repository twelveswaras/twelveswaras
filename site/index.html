How it works

Give it a clip of Carnatic music — from a concert, a recording, or your own singing over a drone.

01 · LISTEN

Record or upload

~15–30 s of melody. At a concert, just point your phone at the stage.

02 · FIND Sa

Detect the tonic

It finds the tonic (Sa) from the tanpura drone and traces the melody line.

03 · IDENTIFY

Name the raaga

A pitch-class fingerprint → the top-3 raagas with confidence, in a few seconds.

Try it

Works best with a tanpura / shruti-box drone under the melody — which a live concert always has.

The recognizer runs on a Hugging Face Space. If the frame is blank, the Space may be waking up — or open it directly.

An open public good

twelveswaras is non-commercial and open source — never a product, not owned by any company. It pairs a raaga-recognition model with a community-contributed, openly-licensed data commons so it improves over time.

The twelve swaras are the twelve note-positions of the octave — the shared alphabet of both Carnatic and Hindustani music. Carnatic is where we start; the name, schema and pipeline are tradition-neutral by design, with Hindustani planned as a fast-follow.

FAQ

Does it work at a live concert?

Yes — a concert has a tanpura drone, which is exactly what it uses to find the tonic. Point your phone at the stage for ~30 seconds. Solo singing with no drone is unreliable.

Is it free and open source?

Yes. Non-commercial, MIT-licensed code, CC-BY-4.0 data commons. Not a commercial product.

How many raagas does it know?

40 Carnatic raagas in this version. Hindustani is planned.

Do I need to install an app?

No — it runs in your browser. On a phone, "Add to Home Screen" for a full-screen, app-like experience.
